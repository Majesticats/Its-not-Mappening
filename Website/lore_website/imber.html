The Endless Chasm

Into the Forgotten Depths

Location: Imber

Imber Overview

Imber is a vast rain-filled cavern where clouds form naturally beneath the mountain roof. Thunder rolls through the darkness, and constant rainfall makes every path slick and dangerous.

Explorers must move carefully through the high humidity and slippery terrain, guided only by lantern light reflecting off wet stone.

Imber is divided into two major regions: the abandoned Sunken Village and the treacherous ascent known as the Thunder Path.

The Sunken Village

This forgotten settlement was carved directly into the cavern walls, with 15–20 homes still clinging to ledges above bottomless pits where rainwater collects.

Wooden bridges sway over the darkness, groaning under the constant drip from the ceiling. Most homes sit deserted, lanterns long extinguished.

Villagers fled after a series of unexplained disappearances—shadowy figures glimpsed during lightning flashes, and the echo of hooves on stone where no creature stood.

The Thunder Path

A steep, spiraling stone trail winds upward toward the higher caverns. Small cave-shops and hut shelters cling to the walls, though few remain occupied.

Waterfalls pour from cracks above, coating the ground in a slippery sheet that makes the climb perilous.

Legends warn that a powerful guardian stalks this route, testing those who attempt to ascend without proving their worth.
